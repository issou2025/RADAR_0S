## Summary

Once #1134 lands, a Zoom recording is watchable directly from our private S3 bucket through the access-controlled in-app player (authenticated view -> presigned S3 URL, reusing the self-hosted `{% video_player %}` path). The manual YouTube upload is then dead weight. This issue removes the YouTube UPLOAD mechanism and its now-orphaned credentials/settings/tests.

## Key decision ŌĆö remove the UPLOAD path, keep YouTube PLAYBACK of existing links

There are two distinct things and only one is being removed:

- The UPLOAD path (REMOVED): the operator-triggered "Publish to YouTube" job that downloads the recording from S3, pushes it to YouTube via the Data API, and writes the resulting URL onto `event.recording_url`. This is what stops relying on YouTube.
- YouTube PLAYBACK of existing links (KEPT, UNCHANGED): many legacy/current events already have a YouTube `recording_url`. `templates/events/_recording_embed.html` still renders `event.recording_url` through `{% video_player %}` as a fallback (precedence: S3 playback URL from #1134 -> `recording_url` -> `recording_embed_url`). Removing the upload mechanism does NOT touch playback ŌĆö every already-set YouTube URL keeps playing.

So the operator can no longer generate NEW YouTube uploads from Studio, but no existing recording stops being watchable. Staff can still paste a watch URL into the recording form's URL field by hand if they ever want to (that field feeds `recording_url` playback, not the upload job).

Full removal is chosen over a feature-flag-off / "optional fallback" because Alexey's stated intent is to stop relying on YouTube, #1134 makes S3 the default watch path, and leaving dead OAuth credentials + a broken-looking button in Studio is worse than deleting cleanly. The YouTube API credentials are only consumed by the upload service, so once the service is gone they are orphaned and are removed from the registry, Studio settings, `website/settings.py`, and docs.
