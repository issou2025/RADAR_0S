*Project description:*
# Research: where the AI-engineering hiring signal is going (74 verified JDs) — and where AgentClash is mispositioned

**Date:** 2026-06-25
**Method:** 5 parallel research agents collected real job descriptions from public applicant-tracking systems (Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby) and company career pages. Every JD below was fetched live; every quote is verbatim; every URL resolves to a real posting. **74 unique JDs** (77 tagged instances incl. 3 cross-segment overlaps) across 5 segments.

---

## TL;DR

The market is hiring, by the hundreds, for one job: **keep MY agent reliable in production, and catch it when a change makes it worse.** Evaluation graduated from a side-skill into a named job family in ~12 months ("Member of Technical Staff, Evals", "Model Behavior Engineer", "Staff SWE – AI Agent Evaluations", "Agent Quality"). The repeated responsibility, across labs, startups, and enterprises, is **eval-as-CI-gate: catch regressions before they ship.**

**0 of 74 roles** describe the job as "choose the best model" or "compare model A vs model B." The unit under evaluation is the company's **own agent's behavior over time**, not a model leaderboard.

That is the core mispositioning: AgentClash's headline is "race models head-to-head with live scoring." That is a benchmark-buyer frame. The 74 JDs describe a **builder** frame: a regression/reliability harness for their own evolving agent, fed by their own production data. AgentClash already has most of the engine for the builder frame (sandbox execution, pass^k/cost-per-success, CI regression gates, immutable event log + replay). It is pointed at the wrong headline.

---

## Method & honest caveats

- **Gated boards were not faked.** Glassdoor, LinkedIn, Indeed, and YC Work-at-a-Startup are login/bot-gated and were **not** used as quote sources. Where a board was inaccessible, it is listed under "Sources we could not access," not guessed.
- **The verifiable backbone is public ATS.** Greenhouse and Lever render JD text directly. Ashby pages are client-rendered (return only a title to a fetcher); agents recovered verbatim text via the **public Ashby Posting API** (`api.ashbyhq.com/posting-api/job-board/<org>`) and Lever's public API (`api.lever.co/v0/postings/<org>/<id>`). The human-facing URLs in the appendix are the canonical postings.
- **A few enterprise own-sites are SPA-rendered** (Stripe, Dropbox, Retool, Grammarly). Those 5 are marked **mirror-verified** (text confirmed on a server-rendered syndication mirror); swap in the canonical company URL if you cite them externally.
- **Theme tags are agent-assigned** from a fixed 15-tag vocabulary, and the frequency table is **directional, not precise** (raw counts over 77 tagged instances; some roles under-tag implicit themes like REGRESSION_CI). Treat the ranking as signal strength, not a survey statistic.

## Where AgentClash is missing the point

1. **The headline targets a buyer that does not appear in 74 JDs.** "Race models head-to-head" sells to model-pickers / leaderboard watchers. The hiring market buys "keep my agent reliable." Re-point the identity.
2. **The CI regression gate is buried; the data says it IS the job.** "Catch regressions before they ship" is the most common responsibility across all five segments. AgentClash has the gate but leads with racing. Invert it.
3. **AgentClash makes you author challenge packs; the market's eval set is its production traffic.** Every segment describes "curate production traces into eval cases." Hand-authored packs fight the grain. Capture-to-eval-set is both the friction fix and the alignment.
4. **Competing on observability is a trap.** "Add production monitoring" as a product surface = a worse Langfuse/Braintrust, who already own it and merged it with evals. Consume OTel; don't rebuild tracing UI.
5. **The execution/sandbox moat is real but mis-aimed.** It is pointed at "race models." Re-aim at "re-execute the user's agent task to verify behavior and catch regressions" — exactly what xAI/Abridge build in-house and regulated verticals need.
6. **Neutral model rankings are a benchmark frame, not a builder frame.** Keep neutrality as a trust property; drop it as the headline. Builders care about their own system, not a fair model leaderboard.

## What AgentClash should do (prioritized, mostly a reframe of existing assets)

1. **Reposition: agent regression firewall / reliability harness.** Headline = "catch agent regressions before they ship." (Already gestured at in #1123.) This is a positioning + landing + onboarding change, not a rewrite.
2. **Capture → eval-set with one key.** Wrap the agent (OTel ingest + thin SDK/proxy); real runs auto-become the regression suite. Removes eval-authoring friction (the real friction) and matches how the market already works.
3. **Lead with execution-based + deterministic verification; offer LLM-judge as table-stakes.** Re-run the task and check oracles (tests/schema/tool-calls/golden) instead of judging vibes. **Fix the trust bugs first** (single-model judge always "high" confidence #1045; regression gate silently passing when it couldn't evaluate #1044) — a reliability product that lies about its own verdicts is dead on arrival.
4. **Make the CI regression gate the centerpiece surface** (PR comment: "this change regressed N cases; here are the failing trajectories"). Re-point existing `run_comparison` from model-vs-model to version-vs-version of the user's system.
5. **Ingest OpenTelemetry; do not build a tracing UI.** Meet teams where their trace data already is.
6. **Keep model-vs-model as a feature, not the identity** ("when you reconsider your model, race candidates on your captured cases"). It rides along on a rare decision.
7. **Premium tier: audit/provenance for regulated verticals** (legal/clinical/tax/accounting — Harvey, Astraea, Abridge, Crosby, Numeral, Fieldguide, Entendre). Use the immutable schema-versioned event model + replay as the latent audit asset. "Eval as the trust artifact you show the board/regulator."
8. **Sell to the Forward-Deployed / founding AI engineer:** code-first, one key, CI-native, no eval team required.
