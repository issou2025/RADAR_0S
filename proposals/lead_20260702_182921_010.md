### Description

This issue was produced by the automated enterprise quality & NFR audit (2026-07-02), which audited the repository against the quality attributes from Wikipedia's "List of system quality attributes" plus AI-native development readiness. The attribute **discoverability** (cluster: Usability & Developer Experience) currently scores **4/5**. Audit justification: Discoverability is strong and partially enforced by automation: README.md carries per-workflow badges and deep links into the docs handbook; docs/README.md is a complete indexed table of contents; `make help` is self-documenting and tests/unit/make_targets.bats asserts every public target appears in it; docs/testing.md maps each local command to its CI workflow. Weak spots are an empty committed CHANGELOG.md, no CODEOWNERS, and downstream-adoption guidance buried inside docs/ci-quality-gates.md ('Maintainer follow-up'). Identified gaps: **Empty CHANGELOG.md misleads consumers looking for release history** — CHANGELOG.md is committed but 0 bytes, while .github/AUTORELEASE.md explains releases are tag-only and the conventional-changelog action runs with output-file: 'false' (.github/workflows/autorelease.yml). A consumer opening CHANGELOG.md finds nothing and no pointer to GitHub Releases, undermining the discoverability of what changed between template-sync updates. **No CODEOWNERS file to route questions and reviews** — There is no .github/CODEOWNERS, so consumers and contributors cannot discover which team owns policy/, pulumi/, or the workflows, and branch-protection review routing (which docs/ci-guardrails.md asks downstream repos to configure) cannot be ownership-based. **Downstream adoption steps are scattered rather than indexed** — The essential post-adoption checklist (mark checks required, configure OIDC role variables, template-sync secrets) lives at the bottom of docs/ci-quality-gates.md and across .github/TEMPLATE_SYNC_APP.md, .github/TEMPLATE_SYNC_PAT.md, and docs/github-actions-secrets.md, with no single 'adopting this template' entry in the docs/README.md contents index.
