## Problem

The workflow actions are **SHA-pinned**, deliberately:

```yaml
- uses: actions/checkout@9c091bb21b7c1c1d1991bb908d89e4e9dddfe3e0 # v7.0.0
- uses: actions/setup-python@ece7cb06caefa5fff74198d8649806c4678c61a1 # v6.3.0
```

That is the right call — a tag can be moved under you, a SHA cannot. But it has a direct consequence: **a SHA pin never updates itself.** Without automation these pins freeze permanently, and the repo silently accumulates actions with known vulnerabilities while *looking* well-secured. The pinning that makes the supply chain safe is exactly what makes it stale.

`.github/zizmor-requirements.txt` is `--require-hashes` pinned and has the same problem.

## Proposal

Add `.github/dependabot.yml` covering:

- **`github-actions`** — the load-bearing one. Dependabot rewrites a SHA pin *and* its trailing `# vX.Y.Z` comment, so the pinning discipline is preserved rather than undone.
- **`pip`** — for `pyproject.toml` dependencies and the hash-pinned zizmor requirements.

Weekly is sufficient; this is not a fast-moving dependency surface (`pyyaml` at runtime, everything else optional).

## Acceptance

- A Dependabot PR for an outdated action keeps the SHA pin format, comment included.
- Its PRs pass the existing gate unchanged (`ci-success`, CodeQL, review) rather than needing an exception.
